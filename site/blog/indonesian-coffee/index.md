## Indonesian Coffee: What is it?
Indonesian coffee, and this may shock you, is coffee grown in and exported from Indonesia. While a bit facetious, this is the point from which I would like to start this discussion. As we will soon discover, that is such an inclusive definition that it almost isn't helpful. Indonesia as a country has several distinct growing regions that produce beans with unique traits, and in a lot of ways their only point of commonality is that they happen to have been grown within one set of lines on a map.

## Recent Market Forces/Trends

Local demand for coffee has skyrocketed in Indonesia in recent years, and that demand is primarily being met by existing local production. This means that less and less Indonesian coffee is reaching global markets. On top of that, the European, and especially UK market, is geared more towards Third Wave roasts, which tend lighter, floral, acidic, etc. These two things combine to make it so that I haven't seen a single bag of coffee from Indonesia in all of my time in Edinburgh. The closest thing I saw was a roast of beans from Myanmar, which was one of the better ones I've had while here.
